George Orwell wrote Animal Farm as a fable, animals overthrowing their human farmer, building a society of their own, dreaming of equality. What follows is one of the most precise and quietly devastating portraits of how power corrupts that literature has ever produced.

The premise sounds simple. It is anything but.

## What makes it fascinating

Orwell's genius is in the lens. By placing the entire social and political power structure of society onto a farm full of animals, he strips away all the noise, the speeches, the flags, the complicated justifications, and shows you the raw mechanism underneath. Who controls the food. Who rewrites the rules. Who gets to decide what the rules meant in the first place.

The pigs start as liberators. They end as something indistinguishable from what they overthrew. And the most chilling part is how gradually it happens. How reasonable each small compromise seems at the time.

## Why I read it when I did

I did not pick this book by accident. My home country Nepal was going through a phase of real political crisis. People in power were neglecting their responsibilities, mismanagement, corruption, no real future being built for the new generation. They kept reaching back to old rhetoric, old stories of past glory that time could no longer justify and that the people could no longer believe.

Then September happened.

A generation, the Gen Z of Nepal, took to the streets. Pure chaos and havoc. But also something rare, genuine collective will. They were able to make the then prime minister step down. Some beautiful souls were lost during the protests. That weight does not go away.

## Where hope lives

Balen Shah now stands as Prime Minister, carrying the promise and the dreams of a majority who dared to imagine something different. I pray that it does not turn into the next Animal Farm. I pray that the opposition chooses the nation over politics. I pray that everyone gets into the same boat, rowing in the same direction, for the development of Nepal.

Orwell wrote a warning. I hope we are wise enough to read it as one.

## Would I recommend it?

To everyone. Especially to anyone living through political uncertainty, which, if you look around, is most of us. It is a short book. It will stay with you for a long time.
